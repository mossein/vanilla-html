015 — december 15, 2024

the last light

The streetlamp flickers twice before settling
into its nightly vigil. I count the seconds
between each pulse of amber light,
wondering if the moths notice
the way I do—

this small death and resurrection,
over and over,
until morning comes
to render us all invisible unnecessary.

notes on this piece
Written at 2am. The streetlamp outside my window has been flickering for weeks. I keep meaning to call someone about it, but now I think I'd miss it.


014 — december 8, 2024

kitchen table confessional

People pray at altars, she said, wiping flour from her hands, but they confess at kitchen tables.

I think about that now, sitting where she used to sit, the wood worn smooth by generations of elbows and forearms, of hands wrapped around warm mugs. The morning light falls the same way it always did.

Some furniture holds memory better than others.


013 — november 29, 2024

three small observations

i.
The spider in the corner of my window has rebuilt her web for the fourth time this week. I admire her persistence, or perhaps her stubbornness.

(Is there a difference?)

ii.
Rain sounds different at 3am. Softer. More honest somehow.

iii.
I found an old photograph today. In it, I am laughing at something just outside the frame. I have no memory of this moment, but I trust it happened.
